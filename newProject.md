# Week 1: How to Make a Prject

## Project Launcher

You will see this screen when you start the Enfusion Workbench. Let’s call it the `Project Launcher.`

![alt text](/images/image-10.png)

The `+ Create New` button is located in the bottom left corner. Or it could also living within the drop-down menu. It is the first option in the image below. 

![alt text](/images/image-11.png)

The `Scan For Projects` is the highlighted option in the image above.   

Here, you can scan your `Arma Reforger/addons` folder for mods that you have downloaded in-game. They will appear with a lock symbol on them. In the image below, you can see the famous red `RHS: Status Quo`  mods with a white lock image.

>Your in-game mods are located in your **Arma Reforger** folder, not your Arma Reforger Workbench folder.

![alt text](/images/image-12.png)
Having your game mods visible in the `Project Launcher` is important for later tutorials. We will demonstrate how to import your game mods in a later tutorial.

You can also find existing projects and  add them to your Launcher.

Select `Create New Project.` to open the project creation interface. In the image below you can see 2 projects. One is a vanilla project that uses only the base game (`ArmaReforger`) and the second has `CaptureAndHold` added. This is called a modded project. However, the two UNTICKED boxes will need to be selected if we want to have access to the mods in the workbench.

![alt text](/images/image-13.png)

A mod with dependencies has addons that are required for the function of the mod. 

Let’s say you make a mod that adds a counter attack feature for `CaptureAndHold.` The `CaptureAndHold` mod is important for the function of your mod. If it is not installed with your mod, the feature is pointless. It also stops players from trying to use your mod with Conflict.

A dependency is automatically downloaded with your mod.

But. Not all mods need a dependency. Sometimes, they can have an addon.

This can be confusing, but we will explain later.

When you are ready, press the OK button and you will find yourself inside the enfusion workbench dashboard.
